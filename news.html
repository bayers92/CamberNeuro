Special Events

In attendance at the 13th International Symposium on NeuroVirology and 2015 Conference on HIV in the Nervous System on June 4-6, 2015, in San Diego, CA.

Dr. Handy Gelbard is delivering the Special Lecture of 2015 Translational Research in NeuroVirology Lectureship, titled as "Inhibition of Mixed Lineage Kinases As A Disease Modifying Strategy for HAND: An Unusually Narrow Path For Patents". The Translational Research in NeuroVirology Lectureship was developed to recognize the contributions of established investigators who study viral infections of the CNS and strive to translate their basic scientific discoveries into clinical applications and ultimately into public health improvements. This year will mark the inauguration of Translational Research in NeuroVirology Lectureship.

Dr. Gelbardâ€™ talk will focus on the historical reasons for investigating the mixed lineage kinases (MLKs) as targets for therapeutic development of new agents that have the potential to modify the disease course of HIV-1 associated neurocognitive disorders. Additionally, the talk will briefly discuss the relationships between a target cell such as a neuron and an immune effector cell from the perspective of MLK signaling. The talk will touch upon the considerable hurdles that exist in shepherding a new small molecule toward a commercial path with the goal of creating a new therapy. Finally, the talk will discuss next steps in identifying other cell types impacted by loss of homeostasis between themselves and immune effector cells, as well as additional targets in the kinome with disease modifying potential.
